13/04 : Dear Data





Dear Data is a year-long, analog data collecting project by information designers and firneds Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec. Living in London and New York City respectively, every week for a year they each collected their personal data on a chosen subject, representing it visually in creative hand-drawings and sending it in a postcard to their counterpart.
I love the analog nature of the artists' drawings, emphasised by the snail-mail delivery. It is refreshing in a world of instant communication and production. Some of their drawings were so decoratively beautiful, and some of the data representation methods so complicated and thoughtful, they were completely detached from the mundane topics they represent, including doors-walked-through, and clocks-checked.

I saw this artwork exhibited in MOMA, NYC in 2022. I was blown away by the paintstaking labor of love evident in every pen stroke.



How many times I picked up my phone this week - Visually Represented



This week I accessed my Screentime settings on my iPhone to see how many times I picked-up my phone each day, and which apps I opened my phone to. These seven columns represent each day of the week, and each square represents an app, with size correlating to number of times I opened to that app. For example, the apps I opened my phone to most were Messages (red checks), and Instagram (blue checks). On thursday I opened my phone to messages 58 times!
